Core antenatal care components include risk assessment, screening tests, health education, and monitoring fetal growth. The first visit should occur before 12 weeks gestation. Routine assessments include blood pressure monitoring, urine analysis, symphysis-fundal height measurement, and fetal heart rate auscultation. Blood tests screen for blood group, antibodies, infections, and anemia. Ultrasound scans assess fetal anatomy and growth. Education covers nutrition, exercise, substance avoidance, and birth preparation.
